About Me
Currently a college freshman at the University of Michigan at Ann Arbor, where I'm studying Aerospace Engineering. I'm interested in mathematics, physics, programming, and engineering.

Current Activities

  • Aerospace Materials Research
    • I entered college with the intention of later getting a graduate degree in Aerospace Engineering. Thus, research was one of my main focuses for my freshman year. After applying to multiple projects through the University of Michigan UROP (Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program), I was able to finally find a project when I directly emailed Professor Henry Sodano. Under Professsor Henry Sodano, I'm currently working as an undergraduate research assistant for Ph.D. Candidate Steven Mamolo. I'm aiming to start my own materials-related research project later this year.
  • Michigan Engineering Zone
    • I wasn't actually planning on becoming a robotics mentor this year, but after attending an initial interest meeting, I knew that it would be the perfect job for me this year. The Michigan Engineering Zone focuses on mentoring FRC teams in Detroit. We help them learn and understand all facets of FRC robotics, including build, design, programming, rules, etc. First Robotics was something that I was especially passionate about in high school, so I'm lucky that I was able to find MEZ this year and be hired as a mentor.
  • MASA
    • MASA is a student-led club at the University of Michigan with the aim of building a liquid-bipropellant rocket engine that will eventually take UofM to space. During my first year, I will be working on propulsion, specifically combustion chamber or valves. This will provide a lot of experience in CAD, machining, and hands-on engineering work, all of which I hope to use for an engineering internship in the summer of 2023.
  • Web Development
    • This website is part of my push to learning some essential programming languages. After starting with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, I plan to continue working on my fluency in Python and Java, implementing those languages into future projects that I undertake. I'll be learning MATLAB this year, as well as C/C++ and Assembly language.
