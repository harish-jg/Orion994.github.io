multiphysics · CFD · HPC

Automotive hood FSI simulation pipeline

Built a coupled fluid–structure interaction pipeline to model stamping defects across 16,000+ automotive hood geometry variants for Ford. Custom versions of OpenFOAM's pimpleFoam and solidDisplacementFoam run in parallel, with projection mapping and data transfer handled by preCICE. The STL-to-displacement-field workflow is fully automated on HPC via SLURM, with cell-count and time-domain optimization cutting total CPU runtime significantly. Python scripts processed simulation outputs into ML training data. Co-authored paper accepted to NeurIPS 2025.

MASA thrust transfer structure

Led the TTS subteam for Michigan's competitive rocketry program. Redesigned the structure to accommodate a 15% thrust increase while achieving a 3% weight reduction. Developed analytical solutions for load paths and failure stresses, including buckling failure mode analysis, then verified against ANSYS FEA. Modeled in Siemens NX with version control through Teamcenter. aerodynamics · CFD · test

Adaptive camber wing

Simulations and wind tunnel testing lead for a senior design-build-test course at Michigan. Built a 3D wing model and reduced to 2D for ANSYS Fluent CFD — mesh refined as a function of chord length, with additional refinement at leading and trailing edges. Ran nine configurations across three camber modes and three angles of attack to extract lift and drag coefficients and visualize flow separation. Wind tunnel testing validated the same matrix. Achieved a 4–8% L/D improvement over conventional flaps. Presented findings to faculty and industry reviewers.

Peltier water cooling system

Designed and modeled a TEC-based water cooling loop for compact electronics packaging. Built a lumped-mass thermal RC network representing the cold plate, TEC, and heat sink, then implemented an implicit backward Euler time-stepping solver in MATLAB for numerical stability at large timesteps. Predicted transient temperatures under variable power loads.
