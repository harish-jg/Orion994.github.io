MASA thrust transfer structure

TTS Subteam Lead · Michigan Aeronautical Science Association

Overview

Led the redesign of a liquid-propellant rocket engine thrust transfer structure for Michigan's competitive rocketry program (MASA). The redesign needed to accommodate a 15% increase in engine thrust while reducing structural mass — a constrained optimization driven by interface requirements from adjacent propulsion and fuel tank subsystems.

Approach & methodology

Developed analytical solutions for load paths and critical failure stresses from first principles, covering column buckling, bearing stress at fastener interfaces, and shear failure modes across all structural connections. These served as the analytical baseline before building the CAD model in Siemens NX and verifying results in ANSYS Mechanical FEA.

Buckling failure mode analysis identified the governing failure mode under combined axial and bending loads from engine gimbal deflection. FEA results were used to validate analytical predictions and refine geometry where stress concentrations were found. Applied GD&T per ASME Y14.5 to all machined interfaces, with version control through Teamcenter. Collaborated with propulsion and fuel tank subteam leads on interface compatibility and load path continuity across subsystem boundaries.

Tools & techniques

ANSYS Mechanical (static structural FEA, linear buckling analysis), Siemens NX (CAD modeling), Teamcenter (PDM / version control), GD&T per ASME Y14.5, manual machining (mill and lathe), analytical structural mechanics (column buckling, bearing stress, shear failure).

Outcomes & results

Achieved a structural design accommodating 15% greater thrust while reducing mass by 3% — meeting both performance targets simultaneously. Buckling analysis confirmed adequate safety margins under worst-case axial and bending load combinations from engine operation. Hardware was personally machined to drawing tolerances on mill and lathe.
