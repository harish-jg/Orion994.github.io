mechanical design · test engineering

In-house appliance certification test stand

Test Engineering Intern · Midea America

Overview

Developed a complete in-house appliance certification testing capability at Midea America, eliminating the company's dependency on expensive external certification agencies. The project spanned the full engineering cycle — apparatus design, prototype assembly, test protocol development, and SOP documentation — creating a reusable internal asset that saves $50K per test cycle.

Approach & methodology

Designed the test apparatus in Creo applying DFMA principles to minimize part count and simplify assembly for repeated use by test technicians. Airflow measurement components were machined in metal to meet the dimensional tolerances required for accurate certification-grade measurements. A representative prototype stand was assembled to validate the design before finalizing documentation.

Authored a complete test protocol specifying setup procedures, measurement sampling methodology, instrumentation requirements, and acceptance criteria. A statistical sampling study was performed to determine appropriate sample sizes and measurement frequencies for defensible certification data. SOP documentation was written for ongoing use by the test engineering team after handoff. Concurrently led an intern group on parallel prototype research and performed thermal simulations of appliance components.

Tools & techniques

Creo (CAD design, DFMA analysis), manual machining (mill and lathe — metal airflow components), thermal simulation, test instrumentation and data acquisition, technical documentation (test protocol, statistical sampling study, SOP).

Outcomes & results

Eliminated dependency on external certification agencies, delivering $50K savings per test cycle. Functional prototype assembled and validated against design intent. Complete test documentation package — protocol, sampling study, and SOP — authored for engineering team handoff and ongoing use. Led intern group deliverables in parallel with primary project work.
